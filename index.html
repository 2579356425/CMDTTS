A Fast and High-quality Text-to-Speech Method with Compressed Auxiliary Corpus and Limited Target Speaker Corpus

Abstract

With the non-target speaker corpus (auxiliary corpus) for model pre-training, Text-to-Speech (TTS) methods can generate high-quality speech under the condition of a limited target speaker corpus. However, this approach comes with high-quality performance and expensive training costs. To overcome the challenge, we propose a fast and high-quality TTS method, which significantly reduces training costs while maintaining the naturalness of synthesized speech. In this paper, we propose an auxiliary corpus compression algorithm ACCA that significantly reduces the training cost while the naturalness of the synthesized speech is not significantly degraded. We then use the compressed corpus to pre-train the proposed TTS model CMDTTS, which fuses phoneme and word multi-level prosody modeling components and fine-tunes the generated spectrograms using denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs). In addition, we introduce a conditional generative adversarial networks (cGAN) to embed the target speaker feature and further improve speech quality using the target speaker corpus. Experiments are conducted on both Mandarin and English corpora, and the results show that the method effectively balances the model training speed and the synthesized speech quality and outperforms the state-of-the-art models.
